Displacement-based design in geotechnical earthquake engineering 
for deep foundations directly links the effects of site-specific seismic hazards to 
operational performance targets. Assessing seismically induced settlement is 
critical for drilled shafts supporting an offshore bridge pier on seismically vul-
nerable marine deposits in Davao, Philippines. However, this assessment is 
complicated by limited code guidance on the service limit state under extreme 
seismic conditions. Furthermore, standard axial spring models are developed 
from static pile tests which do not capture liquefaction or cyclic softening be-
havior. To incorporate these effects to the service limit state, this paper utilizes 
the methodology of estimating pile capacities from target settlements by com-
bining the neutral-plane method with calibrated seismic effects to axial springs. 
Nonlinear site response analysis is performed in DEEPSOIL to generate pro-
files of peak excess pore water pressure to determine effective stress and strain 
reductions in shaft and tip resistances. Liquefaction-induced downdrag and re-
sulting pile-head vertical displacements are then computed in RSPile. Applying 
this framework, site-specific axial pile capacity charts were generated for the 
drilled shafts at the offshore bridge pier as a function of depth and target dis-
placements. These charts account for post-liquefaction reconsolidation and cy-
clic degradation and are benchmarked against code-prescribed strength limit 
state capacities. The study demonstrates the applicability of calibrated axial 
springs to offshore bridge foundations in the Philippine context and provides 
practical guidance for designers evaluating pile capacities at sites vulnerable to 
liquefaction-induced downdrag and cyclic softening. 

The primary objective of this paper is to demonstrate a serviceability-based pseudo-
static procedure for evaluating the axial capacity and vertical displacement of drilled 
shafts under seismic conditions. Specifically, this study will: 

1. Establish baseline static capacities under strength and service limit states. 
2. Integrate 1-D nonlinear site response analysis (SRA) to determine profiles of peak 
excess porewater pressure ratio (ru) and shear strains (γ), and subsequently compar-
ing these with traditional SPT-based liquefaction triggering. 
3. Establish performance targets (i.e. pile settlement) for seismic extreme limit state 
based on literature. This is tied to the bridge pier strength capacities.  
4. Evaluate downdrag forces from force-based negative skin friction demands and 
from displacement-based neutral-plane method. 
5. Compare resistance reduction methods between standard Philippine practices and 
from those evaluated from SRA.
